01 — About

From the cabin to the codebase

For the last few years I've worked as cabin crew for easyJet at Gatwick — a safety-critical, high-pressure environment that runs on procedure, teamwork and staying calm when things go sideways.

Alongside full-time shifts, I taught myself to build software and earned a First-Class Honours BSc in Computer Science with the University of London (academic direction by Goldsmiths). Not toy projects — production-shaped systems: a containerised music-recommendation platform with real-time search, a multi-role learning platform with live chat and a REST API, and a live analytics dashboard built on data from my own industry.

My core stack is Python & Django, backed by PostgreSQL, Redis, Celery, WebSockets and Docker. I care about code that's tested, readable and actually deployed — not just demoed.

Now I'm looking for my first full-time engineering role. I bring reliability, a genuine work ethic, and the kind of grit you only get from earning a degree at 30,000 feet. 🛫
